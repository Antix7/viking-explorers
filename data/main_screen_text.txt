It's year 900 CE. You, the bravest and strongest viking in the village, were chosen to lead a new expedition. The goal: find new, unexplored places beyond the newly discovered Iceland. With spring approaching, it's time to set sail.
Vikings were great explorers because they mastered the art of navigation at sea. They used a tool called a sun compass, which was a sophisticated version of a sundial. They could use it to determine latitude based on the length of the shadow cast by the sundial's gnomon. The sundial had lines carved into it, which represented the position of the shadow at different times of day.
Determining one's longitude would not be possible until the invention of precise clocks in the 18th century, so vikings used a method called latitude sailing. They would sail directly north or south until they reached a desired latitude, then sail straight east/west while keeping their latitude fixed. This allowed them to reach already known locations with ease, but you as an explorer will have to improvise.
You are starting your voyage on the western coast of modern-day Norway. The latitude of Iceland is known to be approximately 65°N. You can turn your ship with the A and D keys, and use SPACE to drop/raise anchor. You can also change the timewarp speed using on-screen buttons or the LEFT and RIGHT arrow keys.
