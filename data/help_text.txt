Your primary method of navigation is the sundial, which consists of two elements: the sun lines and the shadow. A given sun line shows you the position of the shadow throughout the day for a specific latitude. So for example, if you see the tip of the shadow located between the 50° and 54° lines, you know your latitude is somewhere between those values. Of course, the sundial only works during the day.
If you have never sailed before, here's a brief explanation. Sailboats actually reach their top speeds when moving perpendicular to the wind, rather than directly downwind. While you can sail at an angle toward the wind, you cannot sail directly into it. To travel upwind, you must sail in a zigzag pattern, gaining some "height" with each leg and tacking in between.
